  - This is often easier for print-on-demand upload flows than a pre-imposed sheet.

## Print caveat

These files are laid out at a 300 DPI target in a 2:3 card frame, matching the artwork proportions. The source art has been normalized for consistent full-length cards, but the original web card art is still lower resolution than ideal production art. The sheets are good for proofs and first samples. For a polished sellable deck, regenerate or upscale every card directly to the printer's required final pixel size before ordering a full run.

## Recommended production workflow

1. Pick the printer first and download their tarot card template.
2. Confirm trim size, bleed, safe zone, rounded corner radius, and whether they want RGB or CMYK.
3. Order one sample/proof deck before selling.
4. Check tiny title text, border consistency, corner trimming, card darkness, and whether backs align when shuffled.
5. Only then order inventory or connect print-on-demand checkout.
