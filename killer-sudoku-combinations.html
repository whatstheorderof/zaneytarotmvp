Killer sudoku combinations: the complete cheat sheet

Every possible way to fill a cage, for every cage size and sum — with the forced combinations highlighted. Bookmark this page; it does half the work of any killer sudoku for you.

In killer sudoku, a cage's sum limits which digits can live inside it. Some sums are generous — a 3-cell cage totalling 15 can be filled eight different ways. Others are a gift: a 2-cell cage totalling 17 can only be 8+9. Learning to spot the forced ones is the single biggest upgrade you can make to your solving.

How to actually use these tables

  • Start at the extremes. Scan the puzzle for any cage whose sum appears in the forced table above. Pencil-mark those digits immediately.
  • Intersect with sudoku rules. A 2-cell cage summing 16 is 7+9 — but if a 7 already sits in that row, the cage cell in that row must be the 9.
  • Use exclusions. Even a sum with many combinations excludes digits. A 2-cell cage summing 5 (1+4 or 2+3) can never contain 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9 — that's five eliminations for free.
  • Combine with the rule of 45. Cage combinations plus the rule of 45 will carry you through most Hard puzzles without guessing.
